Title


The inspiration behind of the food alphabets is due to my
love for food. When starting on the first few letters of
the alphabet, I thought about how I could make the fonts
look eye-catching and unique, so I decided to use food to
represent letters, since they have different colors, and
there’s a large variety of food I can pick out to represent
the letters in the alphabet. Most of the food I used in
the design are my favourite snacks or food, such as the
chips, sushi and ice cream. During the design process, I
realized that some letters are difficult to represent, such
as the letter “Y”, so I thought about using an ice cream
that somebody took a large bite out of to create the
space between the 2 lines. Likewise, for stacking up sushi
rolls to form the letter “L”.

Mobile Application Portfolio (Adobe XD Prototype)


Originally, the logo was just the mobile phone with the diamond
logo itself, as it represents what the brand does, making apps.
However, I added the charging wire, with the electricity flowing
to the center of the mobile screen app (large diamond logo), as
it gives a sense of feeling that the brand is powering up or
enchancing the app by making it better, which is what the brand
strives for, to improve our apps by improving user experience.

For the colour scheme, I’ve decided to use green, yellow and
blue, which are primary and secondary colours. For the colour
theory, I decided on analogous for the colour blue, which is
the main colour of the app. The blue colour was selected, since
it gives off a technology vibe, as shown in one of the image,
and also represent the brand’s goal to power up the apps, by
constantly improving on the designs and providing user experience,
which is represented by the lightning image. For the typography,
impact was chosen as the font, as the letters are thicken, and
gives off an impactful vibe to the reader, giving the feeling
that every information in the portfolio is important, which will
get their attention and will leave a long-lasting impression.

As for design principles, I’ve worked on the rhythm of the app,
such that elements such as buttons are placed in locations that
indicates that it is interactable. As for repetition, I re-used
the navigation buttons, font, as well as the header for each of
the sub-pages on the app. I’ve also leave plenty of white space
between each element, so that the person looking through the
portfolio won’t be overwhelmed by tons of information cramped
into one small space, especially when it’s on a mobile screen.

The Plant Emporium (Adobe XD Prototype)

The color for the design selected is green and brown, two of the natural colors, and also relates
to the online plant retail website. For the logo, as nature is unbalanced, I decided to put an
unequal amount of leaves of both side of the plant. As the business sells potted plants, the
logo is of a plant in a pot. The tagline "Plant joy, one click at a time" was inspired by the
idea that every click of the purchase button will help others, as a plant will be given to
families for every purchase done. The phrase “Plant joy” is a representation that we are bringing
joy to the family by giving them a plant and the word “Plant” is just a metophorical term used to
describe bringing joy to those families.

Backstory:
Haunted cabin is a single player VR game set inside
a forest, where the player woke up and is trapped in
a small wooden cabin and attempts to escape.

Genre:
The 2 main genres of the game are psychological and
horror, as the gameplay is set in a scary environment,
and the player is constantly in a state of panic and
fear while trying to escape the cabin.

Unique Selling Point:
The game is a combination of a typical horror and
psychological game where the player tries to solve
the puzzles to escape the room.

Art Style:
As the game is mainly in a forest setting, most of
the objects in the building is made of wood, along
with the cabin itself. The cabin will have a dark
spooky theme, and the main source of lighting is
dimly lighted candles scattered around the cabin,
ensuring that important locations are well-lighted up.

Game Loop:
When the game is first launched, the player is spawned
in the middle of a wooden cabin, roughly an area size of
16-meter square. (4x4 meters). The user first must interact
with the objects around the room, and pick up the potted
plant, revealing a yellow key below. This would be the first
puzzle, and the first step to escape the room. Once the user
has the yellow key, they are required to experiment with the
key and realize that they have to use the yellow key to touch
the bottom drawer of the cabinet beside the bed, to open the
cabinet, which will reveal a bucket of water. The player would
be able to grab the bucket of water and has to realize that
they have to touch the fire in the fireplace with the bucket
of water to put it out, and peek into the fireplace, behind
where the fire previous was, which will show a combination
of codes. The player would then interact with a keypad beside
the bed, and enter the correct combination, which will break the
painting, and a red key is behind, where the player can use to
open the door of the cabin and escape. Throughout the game, the
player will be able to click on a button on their handsets while
aiming at the floor, and they would be teleported to that location
for movement.
